# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Parents of roughly 4 to 12 year olds who feel uneasy about how much time their kids
spend on screens and want a low-guilt, practical alternative. They are busy, a little
tired, and skeptical of anything that adds work or lectures them. They browse on a
phone as often as a laptop. The child never uses the site or the app directly; the
whole product is operated by the parent.

## Product Purpose

Questling is a phone app (coming soon to the App Store) that turns screen time into
real-world, screen-free quests. The parent picks a quest, does it with their kid, the
kid earns gems to trade for rewards the parent sets, and photos get saved to a memory
journal. This marketing site exists to explain that loop, build trust before launch,
capture "notify me" interest, and publish helpful screen-time articles for SEO and
goodwill. Success is a parent finishing the page understanding the idea, trusting it,
and either signing up for launch or reading an article.

## Brand Personality

Warm, honest, and calm. Talks to parents like a straight-shooting friend, never a
guilt-trip or a sales pitch. Reassuring ("it's not on you"), plainspoken, first-person
where it fits. Playful about adventure without being childish; the audience is adults.
Three words: warm, grounded, encouraging.

## Anti-references

- SaaS-cream editorial-typographic landing pages (display-serif + mono labels + ruled
  columns). Wrong register for a friendly consumer product.
- Guilt-driven "digital detox" fear marketing. The brand is explicitly no-guilt.
- Loud gamified kid-app aesthetics (rainbow gradients, cartoon mascots, bouncy motion).
  The customer is the parent, so the surface stays grown-up and calm.
- Generic app-landing clichés: hero-metric templates, identical icon-card grids, an
  uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section.

## Design Principles

- **The parent is the user.** Every claim and visual reinforces that the parent stays
  in control and the child stays screen-free. That is the product's whole promise.
- **No guilt, ever.** Copy reassures instead of scolding. "It's not on you" is the tone.
- **Show the real thing.** Use actual app screenshots and concrete quests, not abstract
  promises. Proof over adjectives.
- **Calm and legible over flashy.** Generous space, strong contrast, restrained motion.
  Craft shows through quiet confidence, not effects.
- **Earn trust before launch.** Honest about being pre-launch, privacy-first, and
  genuinely useful (the articles stand on their own).

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Body text meets 4.5:1, large text 3:1. Visible keyboard focus on every
interactive element, a working skip link, and full keyboard operability of the nav.
All meaningful imagery has descriptive alt text; decorative art is `aria-hidden`. Every
animation, including the scroll-driven showcase, has a `prefers-reduced-motion`
fallback. No horizontal scroll at any width; touch targets at least 44x44px.
