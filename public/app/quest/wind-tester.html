The quest
Find out which way the wind is blowing using a ribbon, blown bubbles, or just a wet finger. Run with the wind, then against it. Which way feels faster?
Steps
•
Find which way the wind is blowing
•
Use a ribbon, bubbles, or a wet finger
•
Run with the wind, then against it
•
Decide which way feels faster

Why this is good for kids
Active quests burn energy and build strength, balance, and confidence. A kid who has run, climbed, and jumped is calmer afterward, sleeps better, and reaches for a device far less out of restlessness.
This one is pitched at ages 5–10. With a younger child, keep it short and cheer the attempt rather than the result; with an older kid, add a rule, a timer, or a "do it twice as big" twist to turn it into a real challenge. Either way it is the kind of active play that fills an afternoon with no screen in sight.
