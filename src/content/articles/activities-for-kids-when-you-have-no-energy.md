Some days you've got nothing left. You're flat on the couch, your brain is TV static, and the only thing between you and twenty minutes of quiet is a small person who wants to *do something right now*. The tablet is right there. It's the easy button, and some days you press it. That doesn't make you a bad parent. It just makes you a human one who ran out of gas. Every parent has pressed that button. The goal isn't to never press it; it's to have a couple of easier options within arm's reach.

Here's the good news: **keeping a kid happy when you're wiped out isn't about doing more. It's about setting up one thing that runs without you.** The best low-energy activities aren't the ones where you lead. They're the ones where you point your kid at a mission and let your head fall back onto the cushion. Here are a few that genuinely work, no getting up required. None of them ask you to be the fun one. They just ask you to point.

## Let them build something and disappear into it

Fort building is the closest thing to magic a tired parent has. Hand your kid a couple of blankets, the couch cushions, and maybe a clothespin or two, and they'll vanish into a construction project that can eat a whole afternoon. There's no right answer, so they can't really "finish" and come find you. They just keep making it better. One hour it's a spaceship, the next it's a shop, the next it's a dark little cave they curl up and read inside.

You don't even have to move for this one. If anything, you're the lucky neighbor whose living room is now the site of a major engineering feat. That build-a-hideout instinct is exactly [The Secret Fort](/app/quest/the-secret-fort), a quest that hands them the goal and a few steps to work through on their own.

## Set out one quiet thing and walk away

The secret to a self-running activity is that it's already open and ready before your kid touches it. "Go find something to do" sends them straight back to your elbow. One specific thing, laid out and waiting, pulls them in. The setup costs you two minutes and buys you thirty, which is the best trade in parenting.

- **A painting station.** A few smooth rocks, some paint or markers, and a sheet of newspaper to catch the mess. They'll make an entire gallery of painted creatures, and you'll have done nothing but point. That's the [Rock Painting Gallery](/app/quest/rock-painting-gallery) quest in a nutshell.
- **A sticker book or one big coloring page.** Zero setup, long payoff.
- **A "build the tallest tower you can" dare** with whatever blocks or cups are nearby.
- **A bin of dry pasta and a cup.** Sounds silly. Buys a shocking amount of quiet.

The point isn't which one you choose. It's that you pick *one*, set it down, and let it carry the load.

## Pick the ones you can do lying down

Some activities are low-energy for your kid *and* for you, and those are the real treasures on a no-gas day. Cloud watching is the champion. Send your kid to the window or the back step, or flop down next to them in the grass, and challenge them to spot animals drifting overhead. A dragon. A sleepy rabbit. A whole zoo floating by.

You can do this one with your eyes half closed and still come out a hero. It's basically the [Cloud Zoo](/app/quest/cloud-zoo) quest, and it's exactly the kind of ask-almost-nothing mission Questling keeps on hand. Other lie-down winners: a listening game (how many different sounds can you count?) or "tell me a story about that crack in the ceiling."
