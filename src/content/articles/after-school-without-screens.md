There's a specific kind of tired that hits kids right after school. They've held it together
all day, followed the rules, sat still, and by the time they walk through the door they are
completely spent. So they flop onto the couch, the tablet comes out, and it does not go back
until dinner turns into a fight. If that's your afternoon, you're not doing anything wrong.
That after-school crash is real.

The mistake most of us make is trying to fix it with a rule ("no screens after school") and
nothing to put in its place. That just leaves a wiped-out kid with nothing to do, which lasts
about four minutes. What actually works is giving the afternoon a gentle shape that respects
how drained they are while quietly steering them away from the screen.

## Let them decompress first, on purpose

A kid who just got home is not ready for a big activity, and pushing one is how you get a
meltdown. The first twenty minutes are for landing, not doing.

- **Feed them right away.** A lot of after-school crankiness is just hunger. A real snack
  fixes more than you'd think.
- **Let them be quiet.** Lying on the floor, cuddling the dog, staring into space. Low-key
  and screen-free still counts as rest.
- **Don't quiz them yet.** Save "how was your day" for later. Right after school their tank
  is empty, and questions feel like more work.

## Make the screen-free choice the easy one

When they've refueled and they're ready to move, the tablet will still be the path of least
resistance unless something easier is right there. So make the alternative effortless to
start.

Leave something out and ready: the art stuff already on the table, a fort half-built from
this morning, a ball by the back door. If the screen-free option takes zero setup and the
tablet is in another room, you've quietly tilted the choice without saying a word.

## Get them outside before anything else

If there's any daylight and any way to do it, outside is the single best reset after a long
school day. It burns off the pent-up energy from sitting still, and fresh air genuinely
shifts a bad mood.

It doesn't have to be a whole outing. The backyard, a walk around the block, ten minutes at
the park on the way home. Movement outdoors does more for a crashed-out kid than an hour of
anything indoors, and it makes the rest of the evening go smoother.

## Save screen time for a set slot, later

You don't have to ban screens to end the battle. You just have to move them out of the crash
zone. Instead of "the tablet the moment we're home," make it a known, later slot: after a
snack, after some outside time, maybe half an hour before dinner.

Same amount of screen time, completely different afternoon. Now the tablet is a scheduled
thing they can count on, not the thing that eats the whole afternoon, and there's a lot less
to argue about because the answer is already decided.

The after-school battle usually isn't about a kid who loves screens too much. It's about a tired
kid, an empty stretch of time, and a tablet that's easier than thinking of anything else. Feed
them, let them land, get them moving, and give screens a set time later, and the fight mostly
just fades away.
