The fastest way to burn off a kid's energy indoors is to turn the living room into a
movement space with stuff you already own: a cushion obstacle course, a "hot lava"
floor, indoor bowling, balloon keep-up, or a dance freeze game. Each one gets a
wound-up kid moving hard for ten to fifteen minutes, needs no gear or prep, and
resets the mood without a screen. The trick is physical effort plus a simple goal to
chase.

Because when it's raining sideways and your kid has been vibrating since breakfast,
the tablet starts to look like the only answer. That's a fair instinct and nobody's
judging it. But a wound-up kid usually doesn't need a screen. They need to *move*,
and a living room can absolutely handle that if you lean into it instead of fighting
it.

## Build a cushion obstacle course

This is the heavyweight champion of indoor energy-burning. Pull every couch cushion,
pillow, and blanket into the middle of the room and turn the floor into "hot lava."
The rules make themselves: hop from cushion to cushion, crawl under a blanket tunnel,
balance along a couch-cushion bridge, don't touch the carpet.

The trick is to keep it moving. Time a lap, then challenge them to beat it. Add a
"boss level" where they have to carry a stuffed animal through without dropping it.
It looks like chaos, but it's real physical effort, and it eats a solid chunk of the
morning.

## Race like animals

Animal walks are secretly hard, which is exactly why they work. Line up on one side
of the room and race to the other, but only in a chosen animal's style:

- **Bear crawl.** Hands and feet on the floor, hips high. Two laps and even you'll be
  winded.
- **Crab walk.** Sit down, hands behind, belly up, scuttle backward. Great for giggles
  and grip strength.
- **Frog jumps.** Squat low and leap forward. Loud, silly, and genuinely tiring.
- **Bunny hops.** Feet together, small fast jumps down the hallway and back.

Call out a new animal each round so nobody knows what's coming. Three or four rounds
and the wild energy has somewhere to go.

## Set up indoor bowling

Save six empty water bottles or line up some plastic cups, roll up a pair of socks
into a ball, and you've got a bowling alley in the hallway. A hallway is perfect
because the walls keep the ball roughly on track.

Let them set the pins back up between frames. That reset is half the value, it slows
the game to a rhythm and gives you a breather. Keep score if they're into numbers, or
just cheer for strikes if they're not.

## Play dance freeze and balloon keep-up

Two classics that never miss. For **dance freeze**, put on music, everybody dances
big, and when you pause the song they have to freeze mid-pose. Whoever wobbles is out,
or just make it silly with no losers. Pausing the song is a surprising amount of power
in your hands.

For **balloon keep-up**, blow up one balloon and the only rule is it can't touch the
floor. That's it. A single balloon can hold a kid's full attention for a shockingly
long time, and the constant jumping and swatting is exactly the output you wanted.

## Make a game out of floor tape

A roll of painter's tape is one of the best rainy-day tools you own, and it peels off
clean. Stick lines and shapes on the floor and let the layout do the work:

- **A balance beam.** One long straight line to walk heel-to-toe without stepping off.
- **A hopscotch grid.** Numbered boxes to hop through, one foot then two.
- **A "long jump" runway.** Tape marks to leap past and try to beat.

You lay it down once and it fuels a dozen different games. Bonus: your kid will
probably invent rules you'd never have thought of.
