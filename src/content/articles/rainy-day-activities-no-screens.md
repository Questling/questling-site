The rainy day activities that actually work without screens are the ones matched to
how much energy you have left. Low-effort wins like a blanket fort, indoor scavenger
hunt, or drawing the view out the window buy quiet time. When kids need to move, an
indoor obstacle course, freeze dance, or balloon volleyball burns energy safely
inside. With ten minutes of setup, try kitchen science, a cardboard box build, or a
paper airplane contest.

Rainy days are their own special kind of hard. The walls close in fast, and screens
win by default simply because nothing else got suggested fast enough. So here's a
list sorted by what you actually have left in the tank.

## When you have zero energy left

- **Blanket fort.** Couch cushions, a sheet, a flashlight. Kids will happily live in
  there for an hour while you drink your coffee.
- **Indoor scavenger hunt.** Write ten household objects on slips of paper (a
  spoon, something red, something soft) and let them hunt the house.
- **Sock puppet theater.** A couple of old socks and some marker dots and you've
  bought yourself twenty quiet minutes.
- **Draw the view out the window.** Rain, puddles, the neighbor's car. It's oddly
  calming and needs zero setup from you.

## When they need to move

- **Indoor obstacle course.** Couch cushions to crawl over, a hallway to hop
  through, a taped "finish line" on the floor. Time them and let them beat their
  own record.
- **Freeze dance.** Phone, a playlist, and a "freeze" call whenever you need thirty
  seconds of stillness.
- **Balloon volleyball.** A balloon and an invisible net (a strip of tape on the
  floor works fine) burns a surprising amount of energy without anything breaking.
- **Staircase workout.** Little kids will run stairs for the game of it if you
  frame it as a mission: "how many times can you get to the top before the timer
  goes off?"

## When you can spare ten minutes of setup

- **Kitchen science.** Baking soda and vinegar, a homemade volcano, or just letting
  them measure and stir something for dinner.
- **Cardboard box anything.** A big box becomes a car, a boat, a robot costume,
  whatever they decide. Kids are shockingly good at seeing past the box.
- **Indoor picnic.** Spread a blanket on the living room floor and eat lunch there.
  Same food, completely different afternoon.
- **Paper airplane contest.** Fold a few, mark a starting line, see whose flies
  farthest. Simple, and it always ends in a rematch.
