A lot of grandparents feel quietly outmatched by the tablet. The grandkids show up glued to a
screen, and it's tempting to just let them, or to feel like you can't compete with something so
shiny. But here's the good news: you have something no device does. You're a novelty, you have
stories, and you have time. That's a winning hand.

You don't need to be high-energy or tech-savvy to be the grandparent kids remember. You need a
handful of simple, screen-free things you can do together, sized to whatever energy you've got
that day. Here are some that connect without wearing you out.

## You are the novelty

Kids are fascinated by grandparents in a way they never are by their own parents. Lean into it.
Tell them what you were like as a kid. Show them old photos. Teach them a game you played, a
song, a card trick, how to whistle. The point isn't to out-entertain the screen, it's that
*you* are interesting to them in a way an app never will be.

## Low-energy options that still connect

You don't have to run around to bond. Plenty of screen-free favorites happen sitting down:

- **Card and board games.** A deck of cards is decades of entertainment.
- **Baking or cooking** something together, a recipe they can help with.
- **Puzzles, drawing, or a craft** at the kitchen table.
- **Reading together,** or making up a story back and forth.

## Share what you know

Grandparents are full of skills kids find genuinely magical: gardening, fixing things, sewing,
woodworking, fishing, identifying birds, cooking a family recipe. Passing one of these on is
connection and activity in one, and it's the kind of thing a kid remembers for life. You're not
just filling an afternoon, you're handing down something.

## Get outside, gently

Fresh air helps everyone, and it doesn't require keeping up. A slow walk where they lead. Feeding
ducks. A trip to the park where they run and you sit on a bench nearby. Looking for bugs or
leaves or interesting rocks. Low effort for you, big fun for them.

You don't have to compete with the tablet to be the grandparent kids adore. You've got stories,
skills, novelty, and time, which beat a screen every time. A few games, something handed down, a
gentle bit of outside, and you're making the kind of memories no device ever could.
