Mornings have a way of falling apart the second a screen enters the picture. The tablet
comes out "just while I make breakfast," and forty minutes later you're prying it away
from a kid who's now late, dysregulated, and furious at you. The screen didn't save the
morning. It quietly stole it.

Here's the thing about mornings: they run on momentum, and screens kill momentum dead. A
kid who's locked into a video isn't getting dressed, isn't eating, isn't moving toward
the door. So the most useful screen-free rule in the whole day might just be "not before
we're ready." Let's build a morning that holds together without one.

## Make the order the same every day

Kids move faster when they don't have to think. A predictable sequence turns a dozen
little battles into one quiet routine, because there's nothing to negotiate.

Pick an order and keep it identical every single day: bathroom, get dressed, breakfast,
shoes, out. When the steps never change, your kid stops asking "what now?" and starts
running the routine on autopilot, which is exactly what you want at 7am.

## Put the routine on the wall, not in your mouth

If you're the one barking every step, you become the nag, and mornings turn into you
versus them. Take yourself out of it. Let the routine be the boss instead.

- **A picture checklist** works brilliantly for younger kids: little drawings of each
  step they can follow and check off themselves.
- **A simple written list** does the job for older ones.
- **Point at the list, don't lecture.** "What's next on your list?" beats "hurry up" for
  the fifth time, and it hands the responsibility back to them.

## Do the hard prep the night before

Half of a bad morning is decided the night before. Every choice you can move out of the
rushed hour is a fight you don't have to have.

- **Clothes picked and set out.** No wardrobe debates at breakfast.
- **Bags packed and by the door.** Nothing found at the last second.
- **Breakfast plan decided.** "What do you want" at 7am is a trap. Know the answer already.

Five calm minutes at night buys you fifteen frantic ones in the morning. It's the best
trade in the house.

## Give the "screen not yet" rule a clear line

You don't have to ban screens forever, you just have to move them out of the danger zone.
The cleanest rule is a simple one everyone can remember: no screens until you're fully
ready, bag by the door and shoes on. Then, if there's time left, a few minutes is fine.

That flips the whole incentive. Instead of the screen being the thing that derails the
routine, being ready becomes the thing that *earns* the screen. Same tablet, completely
different morning.

A smooth morning isn't about being a drill sergeant. It's a fixed order, a checklist doing
the nagging for you, the hard choices handled the night before, and a clear line on when
screens are allowed. Set that up once and mornings slowly stop being the worst part of the
day.
