Here's a quiet truth about kids and play: the setup does half the work. A kid faced with a wall
of overwhelming, half-broken toys often just asks for the tablet, because nothing there is
inviting. A kid faced with a simple, tidy, reachable set of good toys will usually just... start
playing. You can nudge screen-free play without saying a word, by shaping the space.

You don't need a Pinterest playroom or a big budget. You need less stuff, arranged so it invites
play. Here's how to set it up.

## Less is more (rotate the toys)

The counterintuitive part: fewer toys out means *more* play. A pile of thirty toys is visual
noise, and kids freeze. So box up most of them and leave out a small, appealing selection. Every
week or two, swap some out for ones from the box. Rotating toys makes old ones feel new again and
keeps the space from becoming overwhelming clutter that sends them straight to a screen.

## Make it inviting and reachable

Kids play with what they can see and get to themselves.

- **Low, open shelves** beat a deep toy box where everything's buried and forgotten.
- **Toys at kid height,** so they can start (and clean up) without you.
- **A clear floor space** or a rug that says "build here, play here."

If a kid can survey their options at a glance and reach them, they'll dive in. If everything's in
a jumbled bin, they won't bother.

## Choose open-ended over single-use

Fill the space with toys that can be a hundred things, not one. Blocks, magnet tiles, play
figures, art supplies, dress-up, a basket of loose parts. Open-ended toys grow with the kid's
imagination and get played with far longer than the flashy single-purpose gadget that does one
trick and gets dropped.

## Keep screens out of it

Make the play space a screen-free zone, on purpose. If the tablet lives on the same shelf as the
blocks, the tablet wins. Keep devices in a different room entirely, so the play space is
associated with real play and nothing else. Out of sight really is out of mind here.

## Where Questling fits

A great play space handles the "what's here to do" question, and [Questling](/) handles the "what
should I do right now" one. It lives on your phone and hands your kid a quick, screen-free quest,
a mission they can carry into the play space or out the door, so a good setup gets an extra spark
of direction. They do it, earn gems toward rewards you set, and the space you built actually gets
used.

Setting up a screen-free play space isn't about buying more, it's about showing less: a small,
rotating set of open-ended toys, low and reachable, on a clear floor, with screens kept well out
of the room. Get the space right and you'll find your kid reaching for the blocks before the
tablet, no nagging required.
